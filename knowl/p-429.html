Since the partial derivative \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(x,y)\) is the ordinary derivative of the function \(f(x,y)\) with \(y\) held constant and \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}(x,y)\) is the ordinary derivative of \(f(x,y)\) with \(x\) held constant, we can use all the differentiation formulas from one-variable calculus to find partial derivatives.
