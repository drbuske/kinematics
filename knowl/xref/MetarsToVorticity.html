Exercise 5. From METARs to Vorticity.

Using METAR data, we can estimate the value of \(\eta = \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial u}{\partial y}\) at location \(X\) in Figure 6.3.14.
City Code A: 052152Z 23005KT 10SM SCT045 SCT220 17/07 A3012 RMK AO2 SLP202 T01670067
City Code B: 052154Z 24008KT 10SM FEW030 15/07 A3014 RMK AO2 SLP206 T01500072
City Code C: 052153Z 15006KT 10SM SCT030CB SCT045 BKN140 BKN250 24/21 A3006 RMK AO2 LTG DSNT E AND NW SLP178 CB NW-N AND DSNT E-SE MOV NE T02390211
City Code D: 052151Z 29010G17KT 10SM FEW065 FEW250 17/M02 A3005 RMK AO2 SLP177 T01671022
  1. Estimate \(\frac{\Delta v}{\Delta x}\) at point \(X\text{.}\)
  2. Estimate \(\frac{\Delta u}{\Delta y}\) at point \(X\text{.}\)
  3. Estimate \(\eta\) at point \(X\text{.}\) Be sure to give appropriate units.
Answer.
  1. \begin{align*} \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta x} \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{6\sin(60)-8\sin(30) \ {\rm{kt}}}{200 \ {\rm{nm}}} \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp 5.98 \times 10^{-3} \frac{1}{hr} \end{align*}
  2. \begin{align*} \frac{\Delta u}{\Delta y} \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{5\cos(40)-10\cos(20) \ {\rm{kt}}}{200 \ {\rm{nm}}} \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp -2.78 \times 10^{-2} \frac{1}{hr} \end{align*}
  3. \begin{align*} \eta = \mathstrut \amp \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp 0.00598 - (-0.0278) \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp 0.03378 \frac{1}{hr} \end{align*}
