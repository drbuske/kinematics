Example 1.1.8. Computing CAPE using the Midpoint Rule.

In \(z\) (height) coordinates, CAPE can be found by the definite integral
\begin{equation*} CAPE = \int_{LFC}^{EL} g \cdot \left( \frac{T_p(z) - T_e(z)}{T_e(z)} \right) \ dz \end{equation*}
where \(T_p(z)\) is the air parcel temperature (in \(K\)) at height \(z\) km, \(T_e(z)\) is the environmental temperature (in \(K\)) at height \(z\) km, \(g\) is the gravitational constant (9.8 \(m/s^2\)), and \(LFC\) and \(EL\) are the heights of the level of free convection and equilibrium level respectively.
From a sounding, suppose we know that \(LFC = 5\) km, \(EL = 9\) km, and we know the information from Table 1.1.9
Defining
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle f(z) = g \cdot \left( \frac{T_p(z) - T_e(z)}{T_e(z)} \right)\text{,} \end{equation*}
we find CAPE by estimating \(\int_{5 \ {\rm{km}}}^{9 \ {\rm{km}}} f(z) \ dz\text{.}\) Computing a midpoint sum \(M_4\text{,}\) as illustrated in Figure 1.1.10, provides us a reasonable estimate.
\begin{align*} \int_{5 \ {\rm{km}}}^{9 \ {\rm{km}}} g \cdot \left( \frac{T_p(z) - T_e(z)}{T_e(z)} \right) \ dz \approx \mathstrut \amp \sum_{i=1}^4 f(\overline{z_i}) \ \Delta z \\ = \mathstrut \amp \left[ f(5.5) + f(6.5) + f(7.5) + f(8.5) \right]\cdot 1000 \ {\rm{m}} \\ = \mathstrut \amp 9.8 \frac{m}{s^2} \cdot \left( \frac{10}{258} + \frac{15}{248} + \frac{10}{243} + \frac{15}{233} \right) \cdot1000 \ {\rm{m}} \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp 2007 \ \frac{m^2}{s^2} \\ = \mathstrut \amp 2007 \ \frac{J}{kg} \end{align*}
