Checkpoint 5.1.10. Algebraic Computation of \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\).

For \(z=f(x,y)=9-x^2-y^2\text{,}\) evaluate \(\lim_{\Delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\Delta z}{\Delta x}\) to find \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(1,2)\text{.}\)
Answer.
We evaluate the difference quotient
\begin{align*} \frac{\Delta z}{\Delta x} = \mathstrut \amp \frac{f(x+\Delta x,y)-f(x,y)}{\Delta x} \\ = \mathstrut \amp \frac{(9-(x+\Delta x)^2-y^2) - (9-x^2-y^2)}{\Delta x}. \end{align*}
When we then fix \(y=2\) and let \(\Delta x \rightarrow 0\text{,}\) we find the partial derivative \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(1,2)\text{.}\) That is,
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(1,2) = \mathstrut \amp \lim_{\Delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\Delta z}{\Delta x}\\ = \mathstrut \amp \lim_{\Delta x \rightarrow 0}\frac{f(x+\Delta x,2)-f(x,2)}{\Delta x} \\ = \mathstrut \amp \lim_{\Delta y \rightarrow 0}\frac{(5-(x+\Delta x)^2) - (5-x^2)}{\Delta x} \\ = \mathstrut \amp -2x. \end{align*}
When \(x=1\text{,}\) we see that \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(1,2)=-2\text{.}\) Compare this result to Example 5.1.2 and Checkpoint 5.1.8.
