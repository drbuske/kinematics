Exercise 9. A Changing Heat Index.

Both relative humidity \(R\) and temperature \(T\) are functions of location. That is, \(R=R(x,y)\) and \(T=T(x,y)\text{.}\) Using the chain rule, complete the dependence diagram in Figure 5.3.11 and write out the formula for \(\frac{\partial H}{\partial x}\text{,}\) the rate at which the heat index \(H = H(R,T)\) changes due to a small movement to the east. Hint: Your answer will involve other partial derivatives.
Answer.
\(\displaystyle \frac{\partial H}{\partial x} =\frac{\partial H}{\partial T}\cdot \frac{\partial T}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial H}{\partial R}\cdot \frac{\partial R}{\partial x}\)
