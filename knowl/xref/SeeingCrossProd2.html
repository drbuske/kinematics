Exercise 8. Seeing a Cross Product II.

Suppose \(\vec{w} = \vec{k}\) and \(\vec{v} = 2\vec{i} - 3 \vec{j}\text{.}\) Compute the cross product \(\vec{v} \times \vec{w}\text{.}\) Sketch and label all three vectors (\(\vec{v}\text{,}\) \(\vec{w}\text{,}\) and \(\vec{v} \times \vec{w}\)) on the axes in Figure 4.3.32.
Answer.
\(\vec{v} \times \vec{w} = -3\vec{i} - 2\vec{j}\text{.}\) Both \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{v} \times \vec{w}\) are in the \(xy\)-plane and are perpendicular to each other.
