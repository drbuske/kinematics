Checkpoint 6.5.20. Calculating Horizontal Divergence.

Suppose the following wind data were received from \(100\) km to the east, north, west and south of a station \(B\text{,}\) respectively: \(270^{\circ}\text{,}\) \(8\) m/s; \(300^{\circ}\text{,}\) \(6\) m/s; \(270^{\circ}\text{,}\) \(6\)6 m/s; \(300^{\circ}\text{,}\) \(2\) m/s. Calculate the approximate horizontal divergence at the station.
Answer.
\begin{align*} \displaystyle \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial v}{\partial y} \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{(8-6) \ m/s}{200 \ km} + \frac{(-6\cos(60^{\circ})-(-2\cos(60^{\circ}))) \ m/s}{200 \ km} \\ = \mathstrut \amp 0.00001 \frac{1}{s} - 0.00001 \frac{1}{s} \\ = \mathstrut \amp 0 \frac{1}{s} \end{align*}
