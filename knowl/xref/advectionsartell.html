Exercise 9. Advection in Sartell.

Figure 4.2.22 shows horizontal isotherms near Sartell, MN. The distance between these contours is \(100\) nautical miles (nm). In addition, the horizontal wind vector \(\vec{v}_H\) is from \(330^{\circ}\) at a speed of \(20 \ {\rm{knots}}\text{.}\)
  1. Find a resolution of the temperature gradient vector \(\vec{u}_T\) at Sartell, MN.
  2. Find a resolution of the horizontal wind vector \(\vec{v}_H\text{.}\)
  3. Estimate the temperature advection at Sartell, MN. Be sure to include appropriate units in your response.
Answer.
  1. Remember that the temperature gradient is always pointed in the direction of the steepest increase. So the gradient in this case will clearly point in the negative \(\vec{j}\) direction.
    \begin{align*} \vec{u}_T = \mathstrut \amp \frac{10^{\circ}C - 20^{\circ}C}{100 \ {\rm{nm}}} \vec{j} \\ = \mathstrut \amp -0.1 \frac{^{\circ}C}{\rm{nm}} \vec{j} \end{align*}
  2. Remember to convert the meteorological angle \(\phi_{met}=330^{\circ}\) to a mathematical angle \(\phi_{polar} = 270^{\circ}-\phi_{met}\text{.}\)
    \begin{align*} \vec{v}_H = \mathstrut \amp (20 \ {\rm{knots}})\cos (270^{\circ}-330^{\circ}) \vec{i} + (20 \ {\rm{knots}})\sin (270^{\circ}-330^{\circ}) \vec{j} \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp (10 \ {\rm{knots}})\vec{i} - (17.3 \ {\rm{knots}})\vec{j} \end{align*}
  3. \begin{align*} -\vec{v}_H \cdot \vec{u}_T \approx \mathstrut \amp -\left[-0.1 \frac{^{\circ}C}{\rm{nm}} \vec{j}\right] \cdot \left[ (10 \ {\rm{knots}})\vec{i} - (17.3 \ {\rm{knots}})\vec{j} \right] \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp -1.73 \frac{^{\circ}C}{\rm{hr}} \end{align*}
