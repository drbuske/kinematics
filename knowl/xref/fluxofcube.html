Exercise 6. Total Flux Through a Cube.

Let \(\vec{F}(x, y, z) = 2\vec{i} + 3\vec{j}\text{.}\) Let \(S\) be the surface of the cube shown in Figure 6.4.37, oriented outward. Find the flux of \(\vec{F}\) through each of the following surfaces:
  1. \(S_1\text{,}\) the right side of the cube (\(y = 10\))
  2. \(S_2\text{,}\) the left side of the cube (\(y = 0\))
  3. \(S_3\text{,}\) the front of the cube (\(x = 10\))
  4. \(S_4\text{,}\) the back of the cube (\(x = 0\))
  5. \(S_5\text{,}\) the top of the cube (\(z = 10\))
  6. \(S_6\text{,}\) the bottom of the cube (\(z = 0\))
Then, determine the net flux through the surface of the entire cube.
Answer.
  1. \(\displaystyle (2\vec{i} + 3\vec{j}) \cdot 100\vec{j} = 300\)
  2. \(\displaystyle (2\vec{i} + 3\vec{j}) \cdot -100\vec{j} = -300\)
  3. \(\displaystyle (2\vec{i} + 3\vec{j}) \cdot 100\vec{i} = 200\)
  4. \(\displaystyle (2\vec{i} + 3\vec{j}) \cdot -100\vec{i} = -200\)
  5. \(\displaystyle (2\vec{i} + 3\vec{j}) \cdot 100\vec{k} = 0\)
  6. \(\displaystyle (2\vec{i} + 3\vec{j}) \cdot -100\vec{k} = 0\)
The net flux through the surface of the entire cube is \(300-300+200-200+0+0=0\text{.}\)
