Exercise 12. Resolving a Storm Motion Vector.

Resolve the vector \(\vec{v}_{storm}\) described in Figure 4.1.48 into components using units of km/hr. That is, write \(\vec{v}_{storm} = a\vec{i} + b\vec{j}\) where \(a\) and \(b\) are measured in units of kilometers per hour.
Answer.
\(\phi_{polar} = 90^{\circ} - \phi_{vect} = 90^{\circ} - 236^{\circ} = 214^{\circ}\text{.}\)\(\phi_{polar}\)\(214^{\circ}\)\(x\)\(46.3\)
\begin{align*} \vec{v}_{storm} = \amp 46.3\cos(214^{\circ})\vec{i} + 46.3\sin(214^{\circ})\vec{j} \\ \approx \amp (-38.4 \ {\rm{km/hr}}) \vec{i} + (-25.9 \ {\rm{km/hr}}) \vec{j}\text{.} \end{align*}
