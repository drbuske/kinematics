Exercise 5. Geopotential Heights at \(45^{\circ}S\) on January 20, 2024.

Data from ERDDAP
 27 
https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/erddap/griddap/nmme\_cfs\_v2\_g500\_6h\_by\_time\_lat\_lon.html
representing 500 mb geopotential heights at \(45^{\circ}S\) on January 20, 2024 can be found in the spreadsheet GeopotentialHeights45SJan2024.csv. Figure 2.2.13 illustrates a plot of this periodic data. Here the horizontal axis represents degrees longitude east of the prime meridian and the vertical axis represents the 500 mb height \(Z\text{.}\)
The geopotential height \(Z\) at \(45^{\circ}S\) can be crudely modeled using a single sinusoid of the form
\begin{equation*} a_0 + a_1\cos(\omega \theta) + b_1\sin(\omega \theta) \end{equation*}
for some constants \(a_0\text{,}\) \(a_1\text{,}\) \(b_1\text{,}\) and \(\omega\) where \(\theta\) is a variable representing degrees east of the prime meridian.
  1. Determine the period \(T\) (with units) and the value of constant \(\omega\) (the frequency).
  2. Use the data in the spreadsheet to determine the values \(a_0, a_1,\) and \(b_1\) so that
    \begin{equation*} Z_1(\theta) = a_0 + a_1\cos(\omega \theta) + b_1\sin(\omega \theta) \end{equation*}
    models the 500 mb geopotential height at \(45^{\circ}S\) and \(\theta\) degrees east of the prime meridian.
  3. Use the spreadsheet to determine values of the additional coefficients \(a_2\) and \(b_2\) in the trigonometric polynomial
    \begin{align*} Z_2(\theta) = \mathstrut \amp a_0 + a_1\cos \left( \omega\theta \right) + b_1\sin \left( \omega \theta \right)\\ \mathstrut \amp + a_2\cos \left( 2\omega \theta \right) + b_2\sin \left( 2\omega\theta \right). \end{align*}
  4. Use the spreadsheet to visually verify the reasonableness of your model above. That is, plot both the data and the calculated trigonometric polynomial \(Z_2(\theta)\text{.}\)
  5. Values of \(a_k\) and \(b_k\) (\(k=3, 4, 5\)) for the trigonometric polynomial
    \begin{equation*} Z_5(\theta) = a_0 + \sum_{k=1}^5 a_k\cos \left( k\omega\theta \right) + \sum_{k=1}^5 b_k\sin \left( k\omega \theta \right) \end{equation*}
    are given in Table 2.2.14. Use this to plot the trigonometric polynomial \(Z_5(\theta)\) with these 5 harmonics against the original data. Your plot should resemble that in Figure 2.2.15.
Answer.
  1. \(T=360^{\circ}\) and \(\omega = \frac{2\pi}{360^{\circ}}\)
  2. \(a_0=5610.154\text{,}\) \(a_1=-91.345\text{,}\) and \(b_1=-1.109\)
  3. \(a_2=-8.279\) and \(b_2=69.432\)
  4. The trigonometric polynomial \(Z_2(\theta)\) and the geopotential heights \(Z\) at \(45^{\circ}S\) on January 20, 2024 are shown in Figure 2.2.16.
