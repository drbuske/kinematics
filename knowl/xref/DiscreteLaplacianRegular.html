Exercise 12. Discrete Laplacian on a Regular Grid.

Suppose that a scalar function such as temperature is known at locations on a regular grid. For example, suppose temperature \(f\) is measured at locations 1 unit away from and at a center location as in FigureÂ 6.6.25.
Using forward differences to estimate first partial derivatives, and backward differences to estimate second-order partials, explain how the Laplacian at \(E\) can be estimated using
\begin{equation*} \Delta f(E) \approx f(A) + f(B)+f(C)+f(D)-4f(E). \end{equation*}
Answer.
Using forward differences, we have
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(A) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{f(E)-f(A)}{1}\\ \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(E) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{f(B)-f(E)}{1} \end{align*}
From this, we note that
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2}(E) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(E)-\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(A)}{1} \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp f(B)-2f(E)+f(A). \end{align*}
Similarly,
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}(E) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{f(C)-f(E)}{1}\\ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}(D) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{f(E)-f(D)}{1} \end{align*}
gives
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y^2}(E) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}(E)-\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}(D)}{1}\\ \approx \mathstrut \amp f(C)-2f(E)+f(D). \end{align*}
From this, using backward differences we have
\begin{align*} \Delta f(E) = \mathstrut \amp \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2}(E) + \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y^2}(E) \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp f(A) + f(B)+f(C)+f(D)-4f(E). \end{align*}
