Exercise 10. Temperature Prediction in Cold Spring.

Figure 4.2.23 shows current temperatures observed near Cold Spring, MN. The horizontal wind vector \(\vec{v}_H\) is shown in Figure 4.2.24.
  1. The horizontal wind vector can be written as \(u\vec{i} + v\vec{j}\text{.}\) Determine the values of \(u\) and \(v\) (in knots). Then, convert each of these components to units of km/hr.
  2. Compute the temperature advection at Cold Spring. What will be the temperature at Cold Spring 30 minutes from now?
Answer.
  1. We have
    \begin{align*} \vec{v}_H = \mathstrut \amp (20 \ {\rm{knots}}) \cos (270^{\circ}-330^{\circ}) \vec{i} + (20 \ {\rm{knots}}) \sin (270^{\circ}-330^{\circ}) \vec{j} \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp (10 \ {\rm{knots}}) \vec{i} - (17.3 \ {\rm{knots}}) \vec{j} \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp (18.5 \ {\rm{km/hr}}) \vec{i} - (32.0 \ {\rm{km/hr}}) \vec{j} \end{align*}
    That is, \(u=10\) knots and \(v \approx -17.3\) knots.
  2. Here,
    \begin{equation*} \vec{u}_T = \frac{(-20+21)^{\circ}C}{16.9 \ {\rm{km}}} \vec{i} + \frac{(-21+20)^{\circ}C}{37.7 \ {\rm{km}}} \vec{j}. \end{equation*}
    The advection is
    \begin{align*} -\vec{v}_H \cdot \vec{u}_T = \mathstrut \amp - \left(18.5 \frac{m}{\rm{hr}} \vec{i} - 32.0 \frac{m}{\rm{hr}}\vec{j}\right) \cdot \left( \frac{1^{\circ}C}{16.9 \ {\rm{km}}} \vec{i} + \frac{-1^{\circ}C}{37.7 \ {\rm{km}}} \vec{j}\right) \\ = \mathstrut \amp -1.9 \frac{^{\circ}C}{\rm{hr}} \end{align*}
    Note that this is negative (cooling). This is mainly since Richmond is so much closer than Avon. So, in 30 minutes (i.e., 0.5 hours), the temperature in Cold Spring will be
    \begin{align*} T_{fcst} = \mathstrut \amp -20^{\circ}C + (0.5 \ {\rm{hr}})\left(-1.9 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{\rm{hr}}\right) \\ = \mathstrut \amp -20.95^{\circ}C. \end{align*}
