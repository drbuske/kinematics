Exercise 4. Circulation Around Pentagonal Path.

Circulation \(\Gamma\) around a path \(C\) due to a wind \(\vec{v}\) can be computed via a line integral \(\Gamma = \int_C \vec{v} \cdot d\vec{r}\text{.}\) In this exercise, we estimate \(\Gamma\) around a polygonal path \(C\) that is oriented counterclockwise. To estimate, we view the calculation for a horizontal wind \(\vec{v}\) as a dot product
\begin{equation*} \Gamma = \sum_i \vec{v}_i \cdot \vec{T}_i = \sum_i \| \vec{v}_i \| \| \vec{T}_i \| \cos(\theta_i) \end{equation*}
where
  • \(\vec{v}_i\) represents the wind vector on the \(i^{th}\) segment of \(C\text{,}\)
  • \(\vec{T}_i\) represents a vector tangent to \(C\text{,}\) pointing in the direction \(C\) is being traversed, and having length that of the \(i^{th}\) segment of \(C\text{,}\) and
  • \(\theta_i\) is the angle between vectors \(\vec{v}_i\) and \(\vec{T}_i\text{.}\)
Locations A* through E* on path \(C\) have wind data (taken from METARs) as given in Table 6.2.22.
  1. Fill in Table 6.2.23 with values of \(\|\vec{v}_i\|\text{,}\) \(\|\vec{T}_i\|\text{,}\) and \(\theta_i\) along path \(C\text{.}\) Values for locations \(D^*\) and \(E^*\) are provided.
  2. Calculate, approximately, the circulation \(\Gamma\) for \(C\text{.}\) Be sure to include units.
Answer.
  1. The angle between the direction of the wind and the tangent to \(C\) is the difference between \(90^{\circ}-\)Wind Direction given and the direction of motion.
  2. \begin{align*} \Gamma \approx \mathstrut \amp (20 \ KT)(0.1 \ NM)\cos(210^{\circ}) + (15 \ KT)(0.5 \ NM)\cos(126.9^{\circ}) \\ \mathstrut \amp + (12 \ KT)(0.1 \ NM)\cos(0^{\circ}) + (8 \ KT)(0.4 \ NM)\cos(0^{\circ})\\ \mathstrut \amp + (10 \ KT)(0.5 \ NM)\cos(120^{\circ})\\ = \mathstrut \amp -3.603 \ \frac{nm^2}{hr} \end{align*}
