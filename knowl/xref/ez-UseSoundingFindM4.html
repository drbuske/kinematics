Checkpoint 1.1.11. Use a Sounding and a Right Sum to Estimate CAPE.

Use the sounding in Figure 1.1.12 and the right sum \(R_6\) to estimate CAPE.
Answer.
The LFC for this sounding appears to be at roughly 500 meters and the EL appears to be at roughly 1250 meters. Using \(\Delta z = 2\) km, we can construct the values found in Table 1.1.13.
We use a right Riemann sum, \(R_6\text{,}\) to estimate
\begin{equation*} \int_{0.5 \ {\rm{km}}}^{12.5 \ {\rm{km}}} g \cdot \left( \frac{T_p(z) - T_e(z)}{T_e(z)} \right) \ dz. \end{equation*}
To simplify, we define \(f(z) = g \cdot \left( \frac{T_p(z) - T_e(z)}{T_e(z)} \right)\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} R_6 = \mathstrut \amp \sum_{i=1}^6 f(z_i) \ \Delta z \\ = \mathstrut \amp \left[ f(2.5) + f(4.5) + f(6.5) + f(8.5) + f(10.5) + f(12.5) \right] \cdot 2 \ {\rm{km}} \\ = \mathstrut \amp 9.8 \ \frac{\rm{m}}{{\rm{s}}^2} \cdot \left( \frac{17}{266} + \frac{16}{256} + \frac{6}{251} + \frac{9}{236} + \frac{5}{222} + \frac{0}{215} \right) \cdot 2000 \ {\rm{m}} \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp 4135 \ \frac{\rm{J}}{\rm{kg.}} \end{align*}
This right Riemann sum \(R_6\) is illustrated in Figure 1.1.14.
