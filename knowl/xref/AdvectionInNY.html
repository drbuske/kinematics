Exercise 8. Advection in New York.

Figure 4.2.21 shows temperatures observed at four New York stations at 2000 UTC 21 February 2018 as a strong cold front was moving through the area.
  1. Calculate the temperature gradient vector \(\vec{u}_T\) at Glenville, NY. Be sure to include appropriate units.
  2. The horizontal wind vector at this time was
    \begin{equation*} \vec{v}_H = (29 \ {\rm{km/hr}})\vec{i} -(7\ {\rm{km/hr}}) \vec{j}. \end{equation*}
    Estimate the temperature advection at Glenville, NY. Be sure to include units in your response.
Answer.
  1. \begin{gather*} \vec{u}_T = \frac{ 20.6^{\circ}C - 8.9^{\circ}C}{87 \ {\rm{km}} }\vec{i} + \frac{14.4^{\circ}C - 19.4^{\circ}C}{65 \ {\rm{km}} }\vec{j} \\ = 0.1345 \frac{ ^\circ C}{\rm{km}} \vec{i} -0.0769 \frac{^{\circ}C}{\rm{km}}\vec{j} \end{gather*}
  2. \begin{align*} -\vec{v}_H \cdot \vec{u}_T = \mathstrut \amp -\left[ (29 \ {\rm{km/hr}})\vec{i} -(7 \ {\rm{km/hr}})\vec{j}\right] \cdot \left[ 0.1345 \frac{^{\circ}C}{\rm{km}}\vec{i} -0.0769 \frac{^{\circ}C}{\rm{km}}\vec{j}\right] \\ = \mathstrut \amp -4.4388 \frac{^\circ C}{\rm{hr}} \end{align*}
