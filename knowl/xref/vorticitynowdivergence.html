Exercise 5. Horizontal Divergence at a Point.

In Checkpoint 6.3.12, we estimated the vertical component of vorticity at the point in Figure 6.5.23. Now, estimate the horizontal divergence at this point. The dotted square is \(100 \ m\) by \(100 \ m\text{.}\) Each vector has magnitude \(10 \ m/s\text{.}\)
Answer.
\begin{align*} \displaystyle \nabla_H \cdot \vec{v}_H = \mathstrut \amp \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial v}{\partial y}\\ = \mathstrut \amp \frac{\Delta u}{\Delta x} + \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta y}\\ = \mathstrut \amp \frac{10 \ m/s - 0 \ m/s}{100 \ m} + \frac{10 \sin(45^{\circ}) - 10\sin(60^{\circ}) \ m/s}{100 \ m}\\ \approx \mathstrut \amp 0.1 -0.016 \ \frac{1}{s} \\ = \mathstrut \amp 0.084 \ \frac{1}{s}. \end{align*}
The positive answer suggests convergence at the point.
