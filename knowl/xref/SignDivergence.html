Example 6.5.3. Sign of Divergence.

For the vector field \(\vec{F}\) illustrated in Figure 6.5.4, the divergence at the origin is negative since there is a net flow into that point. All of the flow appears to be towards the origin as it passes through the surface of the sphere. If the surface of this sphere \(S\) were oriented outward, then the flux of \(\vec{F}\) across \(S\) would be negative. Hence the flux density (i.e. the divergence) at the origin is also negative.
For the vector field \(\vec{F}\) illustrated in Figure 6.5.5, the divergence at the origin is positive since there is a net flow out of that point. While a small amount of the flow appears to be moving towards the origin from the left, much more flow is moving away towards the right. If the surface of this sphere \(S\) were oriented outward, then the flux of \(\vec{F}\) across \(S\) (no matter the size of \(S\text{!}\)) would be positive. The divergence, or flux density, at the origin is then also positive.
