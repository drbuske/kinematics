Example 6.5.18. Calculating Horizontal Divergence.

The following wind data were received from \(50\) km to the east, north, west and south of a station \(A\text{,}\) respectively: \(90^{\circ}\text{,}\) \(10\) m/s; \(120^{\circ}\text{,}\) \(4\) m/s; \(90^{\circ}\text{,}\) \(8\) m/s; \(60^{\circ}\text{,}\) \(4\) m/s. We calculate the approximate horizontal divergence at the station.
The horizontal divergence can be approximated by
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial v}{\partial y} \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{\Delta u}{\Delta x} + \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta y}\\ = \mathstrut \amp \frac{[-10 - (-8)]\ m/s}{100\ km} + \frac{[2-(-2)]\ m/s}{100\ km}\\ = \mathstrut \amp \frac{2 \ m/s}{100,000\ m} \\ = \mathstrut \amp 2 \times 10^{-5} \frac{1}{s}. \end{align*}
Note the units of divergence are 1/s.
