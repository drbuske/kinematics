Example 5.1.6. Numerical Estimation of the Partial Derivative.

Values of \(z = 9-x^2-y^2\) near \(x=1\text{,}\) \(y=2\) appear in Table 5.1.7.
Estimating the rate at which \(z\) changes with respect to a change in \(y\) (while keeping \(x\) fixed at a value of 1), using a forward difference from the data in Table 5.1.7 suggests that
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \frac{\partial z}{\partial y}(1,2) \approx \frac{\Delta z}{\Delta y} = \frac{3.59 - 4}{2.1 - 2} = -4.1. \end{equation*}
This is similar to the instantaneous rate of change \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\) calculated in Checkpoint 5.1.4. It should be noted too that other reasonable estimates on this rate can also be made from Example 5.1.6. For example, the central difference
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \frac{\partial z}{\partial y}(1,2) \approx \frac{\Delta z}{\Delta y} = \frac{3.59 - 4.39}{2.1 - 1.9} = -4, \end{equation*}
(a seemingly very good estimate!) incorporates behavior of \(z\) on both sides of \(y=2\text{.}\)
