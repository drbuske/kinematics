Exercise 13. The \(\vec{k}\) Component.

The dotted lines in Figure 4.3.37 form a \(4 \times 5 \times 7\) box.
  1. Resolve the vector \(\vec{u}\) into its components.
  2. Is the dot product \(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{k}\) positive, negative, or zero?
  3. Compute \(\vec{u} \times \vec{k}\text{.}\)
Answer.
  1. \(\displaystyle \vec{u} = -5\vec{i} + 7\vec{j} - 4\vec{k}\)
  2. negative
  3. \(\displaystyle 7\vec{i} + 5\vec{j}\)
