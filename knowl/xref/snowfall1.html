Checkpoint 3.1.8. Snowfall.

Consider \(S = g(d, T)\) where \(S\) represents newly fallen snowfall (in inches) as a function of the melt water depth \(d\) (in inches) and temperature \(T\) (in \(^\circ F\)).
  1. Compute \(S = g(0.5 \ \rm{inches}, 5^{\circ}F)\text{.}\)
  2. If it is known that 10 inches of snowfall occurred, can the melt water depth \(d\) be uniquely determined? If so, what is this value? If not, explain why not.
  3. If \(d\) is held constant, is the single variable function that results increasing, decreasing, or neither?
Answer.
  1. 20 inches (of snowfall)
  2. The value of \(d\) cannot be determined. More than one combination of melt water depth \(d\) and temperature \(T\) could have produced this snowfall. For example, \(g(1,30)=g(0.5,17)=g(0.25,5)=10\text{.}\)
  3. For a given melt water depth \(d\text{,}\) as the temperature \(T\) increases, the resulting snowfall decreases.
