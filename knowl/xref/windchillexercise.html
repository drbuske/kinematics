Checkpoint 3.1.6. Wind Chill.

Wind chill is a temperature which tells you how cold it feels as a result of the combination of wind and temperature. Let \(C = f(w,T)\) where \(C\) is the wind chill temperature (in degrees Fahrenheit) that is associated with a wind speed of \(w\) miles per hour and a temperature of \(T\) degrees Fahrenheit. Values for the function \(f\) are given in TableÂ 3.1.7.
  1. Evaluate and interpret \(f(20,5)\text{.}\)
  2. How fast does the wind need to blow for it to feel like \(-10^{\circ}F\) when the air temperature is really \(5^{\circ}F\text{?}\)
  3. If \(w\) is held constant, is the single variable function you get increasing, decreasing, or neither?
  4. If \(T\) is held constant, is the single variable function you get increasing, decreasing, or neither?
Answer.
  1. \(f(20,5) = -15^{\circ}F\text{;}\) When the wind speed is 20 mph and the temperature is \(5^{\circ}F\text{,}\) the windchill (or feels like temperature) is \(-15^{\circ}F\text{.}\)
  2. 10 mph
  3. increasing
  4. decreasing
