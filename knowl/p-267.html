The mean wind is defined to be the average of the vectors \(\vec{v}_{\rm{sfc}}\) and \(\vec{v}_{5 {\rm{km}}}\text{.}\) That is,
\begin{gather*} \vec{v}_{\rm{mean}} = \frac{1}{2}\vec{v}_{\rm{sfc}} + \frac{1}{2}\vec{v}_{5 {\rm{km}}}. \end{gather*}

  2. Resolve vectors \(\vec{v}_{\rm{sfc}}\) and \(\vec{v}_{5 {\rm{km}}}\) into components. Then, use the resolutions to write the mean wind \(\vec{v}_{\rm{mean}}\) as \(u\vec{i} + v\vec{j}\) for some constants \(u\) and \(v\text{.}\)
  3. In a supercell thunderstorm, there can be propagation due to the rotating updraft (called updraft-shear propagation).
