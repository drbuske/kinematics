By the chain rule, note that
\begin{equation*} \frac{dW}{dt} = \frac{\partial W}{\partial T}\frac{dT}{dt} + \frac{\partial W}{\partial v}\frac{dv}{dt}. \end{equation*}
Since
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial W}{\partial T}= 0.6215 + 0.3965v^{0.16} \ {\rm{and}} \ \frac{\partial W}{\partial v}= (0.16)(11.37)v^{-0.84} + (0.16)(0.3965)Tv^{-0.84} \end{equation*}
we have
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial W}{\partial T}(-15,30) = 0.6215+0.3965(30)^{0.16} \approx 1.305 \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial W}{\partial v}(-15,30) = (0.16)(11.37)(30)^{-0.84} + (0.16)(0.3965)(-15)(30)^{-0.84} \approx 0.05. \end{equation*}
So at 11:00 a.m. (when \(T = -15^{\circ}C\) and \(v = 30\) km/hr) we have
\begin{align*} \frac{dW}{dt} = \mathstrut \amp \frac{\partial W}{\partial T}\frac{dT}{dt} + \frac{\partial W}{\partial v}\frac{dv}{dt} \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp \left(1.305 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{^{\circ}C} \right) \cdot \left( -4 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{hr}\right) + \left( 0.05 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{km/hr} \right) \cdot \left( 2 \ \frac{km/hr}{hr}\right) \\ = \mathstrut \amp -5.22 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{hr} + 0.1 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{hr} \\ = \mathstrut \amp -5.12 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{hr}. \end{align*}
This means that the wind chill (or feels like temperature) is dropping at a rate of 5.12 degrees Celsius per hour at 11:00 a.m.. Most of the contribution to this drop is coming from the rapid drop in ambient temperature \(T\text{.}\)
