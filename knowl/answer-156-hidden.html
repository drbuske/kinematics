1. First, we estimate \(\frac{\partial I}{\partial T}\) at two locations near \((94,75)\text{.}\) For example,
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\partial I}{\partial T}(94,75) \approx \frac{130-122}{96-94} = 4 \frac{^{\circ}F}{^{\circ}F} \end{equation*}
    and
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\partial I}{\partial T}(92,75) \approx \frac{122-115}{94-92} = 3.5 \frac{^{\circ}F}{^{\circ}F}. \end{equation*}
    Then,
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\partial^2 I}{\partial T^2}(94,75) \approx \frac{\frac{\partial I}{\partial T}(94,75)-\frac{\partial I}{\partial T}(92,75)}{94-92} \approx \frac{4-3.5}{94-92} = 0.25 \frac{^{\circ}F/^{\circ}F}{^{\circ}F}. \end{equation*}
    When the temperature is 94 degrees and the relative humidity is 75%, this value tells us how fast the rate of change of the heat index is increasing as temperature increases.
  2. This time estimate \(\frac{\partial I}{\partial H}\) at two locations near \((94,75)\text{:}\)
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\partial I}{\partial H}(94,75) \approx \frac{127-122}{80-75} = 1 \frac{^{\circ}F}{\%} \end{equation*}
    and
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\partial I}{\partial H}(94,70) \approx \frac{122-118}{75-70} = 0.8 \frac{^{\circ}F}{\%}. \end{equation*}
    Then,
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\partial^2 I}{\partial H^2}(94,75) \approx \frac{\frac{\partial I}{\partial H}(94,75)-\frac{\partial I}{\partial H}(94,70)}{75-70} \approx \frac{1-0.8}{5} = 0.04 \frac{^{\circ}F/\%}{\%}. \end{equation*}
    When the temperature is 94 degrees and the relative humidity is 75%, this value tells us how fast the rate of change of the heat index is increasing as the relative humidity increases.
  3. To estimate the mixed partial, we use values
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\partial I}{\partial H}(94,75) \approx \frac{127-122}{80-75} = 1 \frac{^{\circ}F}{\%} \end{equation*}
    and
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\partial I}{\partial H}(92,75) \approx \frac{119-115}{80-75} = 0.8 \frac{^{\circ}F}{\%}. \end{equation*}
    Then,
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\partial^2 I}{\partial T \partial H}(94,75) \approx \frac{\frac{\partial I}{\partial H}(94,75)-\frac{\partial I}{\partial H}(92,75)}{94-92} \approx \frac{1-0.8}{2} = 0.1 \frac{^{\circ}F/\%}{^{\circ}F}. \end{equation*}
    When the temperature is 94 degrees and the relative humidity is 75%, this value tells us how fast the rate of change of the heat index with respect to a change in humidity is increasing as the temperature increases.
