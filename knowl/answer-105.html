Answer 3.1.23.1.

  1. \(C(10,25) \approx 15.05^{\circ}F\) which is quite similar to the value of \(15^{\circ}F\) found in Table 3.1.21.
  2. \(\displaystyle \frac{\partial C}{\partial w} = -\frac{5.72}{w^{0.84}} + \frac{0.0684T}{w^{0.84}}\text{;}\) \(\displaystyle \frac{\partial C}{\partial T} = 0.6215 + 0.4275w^{0.16}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \frac{\partial C}{\partial w}(10,25) \approx -0.58^{\circ}F/mph\text{;}\) In practical terms, this gives the rate at which the wind chill is changing when the wind speed is \(w=10\) mph and the air temperature is \(T=25^{\circ}F\text{.}\) Under those conditions, when the velocity of the wind increases 1 mph, we can expect the feels like temperature (i.e. wind chill) to decrease by about 0.6 of a degree Fahrenheit even if the air temperature remains constant. This is roughly the same as in Checkpoint 3.1.20(a).
