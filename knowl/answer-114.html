Answer 3.1.3.8.1.

  1. \(f(25,-20)=7\text{.}\) This means that when the wind speed is 25 mph and the air temperature is -20 degrees Fahrenheit, frostbit will happen in 7 minutes.
  2. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial v}(25,-20) \approx \frac{6-8}{30-20} = -0.2\) minutes per mph.
  3. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial v}(25,-20) = -0.2\) minutes per mph means that when the temperature is -20\(^{\circ}F\) and the wind speed is 25 mph, the rate at which frostbite occurs is reduced by 0.2 minutes for every 1 mph increase in wind speed.
