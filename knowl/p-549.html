Vorticity in fluids is similar to angular momentum in solids. Just like curl, it is easiest to visualize vorticity by thinking of a small paddle wheel immersed in the fluid. If the fluid flow turns the paddle wheel, then it has vorticity. Vorticity is a vector, and points out of the plane in which the fluid turns. If you curl the fingers on your right hand in the direction of the turning paddle wheel and your thumb points upward, then the vorticity is positive. If your thumb points downward, the vorticity is negative. Does this sound familiar? It should! If not, review the concept of curl in the last section.
