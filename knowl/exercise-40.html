Checkpoint 2.3.25. Coriolis Force.

Suppose that we are on the surface of the Earth (outside of the science building) and hit a golf ball due south with speed \(v\text{.}\)

  1. Explain why the magnitude of the Coriolis force \(|\vec{F}_c|\) has value

    \begin{equation*} 2mv\Omega \sin\varphi \end{equation*}

    where

    • \(\Omega\) is the rotation rate of the Earth \(\Omega = 7.2921 \times 10^{-5}\) rad/s and

    • \(\varphi\) is the angle which ranges from \(0^\circ\) at the Equator to \(90^\circ\) (North or South) at the poles and specifies the north-south position of the location.

  2. The effect is very slight. To see this, calculate the magnitude of the force \(\vec{F}_g\) on the golf ball due to gravity and compare it (as a percentage) to the magnitude of the Coriolis force. Assume the mass of the golf ball is \(m= 45\) g, a typical ball speed \(v=\) 50 m/s, and a latitude of \(\phi = 45.6^{\circ}\text{.}\) [Hint: You are calculating \(|\vec{F}_c|/|\vec{F}_g|\text{.}\)]
