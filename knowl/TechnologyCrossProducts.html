Exercise 11. Technology and Vector Products.

Technology can really help with computation of cross products. Try it!
  1. Pick two vectors \(\vec{a}\) and \(\vec{b}\) at random. Use your favorite technology (such as Wolfram Alpha) to compute \((\vec{a} \times \vec{b})\cdot \vec{a}\) and \((\vec{a} \times \vec{b})\cdot \vec{b}\text{.}\) Hint: In Wolfram Alpha, try typing something like ( <1,2,3> cross <4,5,6>) dot <1,2,3> .
  2. Would the above result be obvious by geometric or algebraic computation?
