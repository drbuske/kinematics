The wind chill index is modeled by the function
\begin{equation*} W(T,v) = 13.12 + 0.6215T - 11.37v^{0.16} + 0.3965Tv^{0.16} \end{equation*}
where \(T\) is the temperature in degrees Celsius and \(v\) is the wind speed (in km/hr). The temperature \(T\) and the wind speed \(v\) are also, of course, functions of time \(t\text{.}\) Suppose that at 11:00 a.m., we have \(T = -15^{\circ}C\) and \(v = 30\) km/hr. Further, suppose that \(T(t)\) is decreasing by \(^{\circ}C/hr\) and \(v(t)\) is increasing by 2 km/hr. That is, \(\frac{dT}{dt} = -4 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{hr}\) and \(\frac{dv}{dt} = 2 \ \frac{km/hr}{hr}\text{.}\) At what rate is the apparent temperature (i.e. the wind chill \(W\)) changing at 11:00 a.m.?
