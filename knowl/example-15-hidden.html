FigureÂ 2.2.16 shows current temperatures observed near Rochester, MN. Suppose the horizontal wind vector (with units of m/s) is \(\vec{v}_H = 10\vec{i} -3 \vec{j}.\)

Here,

\begin{equation*} \vec{u}_T = \frac{(8.2-9.1)^{\circ}C}{153,000m} \vec{i} + \frac{(8.7-8.5)^{\circ}C}{56,000m}\vec{j}. \end{equation*}

It follows that the temperature advection at Rochester is

\begin{align*} -\vec{v}_H \cdot \vec{u}_T = \mathstrut \amp -(10\frac{m}{\sec}\vec{i} -3\frac{m}{\sec} \vec{j}) \cdot \left(\frac{-0.9^{\circ}C}{153,000m} \vec{i} + \frac{0.2^{\circ}C}{56,000m}\vec{j}\right) \\ = \mathstrut \amp \frac{9^{\circ}C}{153,000\sec} + \frac{0.6^{\circ}C}{56,000\sec} \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp 0.000070\frac{^{\circ}C}{\sec} \end{align*}

If the current temperature at Rochester is \(8.5^{\circ}C\text{,}\) the temperature 2 hours from now is given by

\begin{align*} T_{fcst} = \mathstrut \amp T_{curr} - (\Delta t) \vec{v}_H \cdot \vec{u}_T\\ = \mathstrut \amp 8.5^{\circ}C - (2 \ hr)\left(\frac{60 \min}{1 \ hr}\right)\left(\frac{60 \sec}{1 \min}\right) \left( -0.000070\frac{^{\circ}C}{\sec}\right) \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp 9.0^{\circ}{C}\text{.} \end{align*}
