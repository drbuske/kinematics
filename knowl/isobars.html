Example 1.3.2. Isobars.

Atmospheric pressure, a function of location, is measured in millibars. The map in Figure 1.3.3 shows the sea-level pressures for various locations.
In Figure 1.3.4, lines of equal values have been connected and labeled (using only the last two digits of the millibar measurement). These level curves for atmospheric pressure are called isobars. Isobars can be used to identify "Highs" and "Lows". These too have been labeled in Figure 1.3.4.
