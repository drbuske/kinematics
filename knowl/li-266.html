Item 2.3.27.b.
The Coriolis effect is very slight. To see this, calculate the magnitude of the force on the golf ball \(\vec{F}_g\) due to gravity and compare it (as a percentage) to the magnitude of the Coriolis force \(\vec{F}_{\rm{Cor}}\text{.}\) Assume the mass of the golf ball is \(m=45\) g and travels at a typical ball speed of \(v=\) 50 m/s. [Hint: You are calculating \(\|\vec{F}_{\rm{Cor}}\|/\|\vec{F}_g\|\text{.}\)]
