  1. Geometrically, the mean wind lies exactly between the surface wind and the 5 km wind.
  2. \(\vec{v}_{\rm{sfc}} = \vec{i} + 2\vec{j}\) and \(\vec{v}_{5 {\rm{km}}} = 5\vec{i} + 2\vec{j}\text{.}\) So,
    \begin{align*} \vec{v}_{\rm{mean}} = \amp \frac{1}{2}(\vec{i} + 2\vec{j}) + \frac{1}{2}(5\vec{i} + 2\vec{j}) \\ = \amp 3\vec{i} + 2 \vec{j}. \end{align*}
  3. Note that \(\vec{v}_{\rm{mean}} = 8\vec{i} + 8\vec{j}\) and \(\vec{v}_{usp} = 1\vec{j}\text{.}\) This makes \(\vec{v}_{\rm{storm}} = \vec{v}_{\rm{mean}} + \vec{v}_{usp} = 8\vec{i} + 9\vec{j}\text{.}\) The meteorlogical direction will then be given by \(\phi_{met} = 270^{\circ}-\arctan(8/9) \approx 228.4^{\circ}\text{.}\)
