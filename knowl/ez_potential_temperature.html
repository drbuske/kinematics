Exercise 6. Potential Temperature.

\begin{equation*} \theta(T,p) = T \left( \frac{1000}{p} \right)^{0.286}. \end{equation*}
  1. Compute \(\theta(280,1000)\text{.}\) What units does this value have?
  2. When \(p < 1000\) (as it is in the atmosphere) is the potential temperature \(\theta\) greater or less than the absolute temperature \(T\text{?}\) What happens as \(p\) decreases (as a rising air parcel does in the atmosphere)?
  3. Compute \(\frac{\partial \theta}{\partial T}(T,p)\text{.}\) What does this calculation say about how the potential temperature changes when holding pressure \(p\) constant?
  4. Compute \(\frac{\partial \theta}{\partial p}(T,p)\text{.}\) What does it mean to have this value be negative?
