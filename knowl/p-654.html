To estimate the second-order partial \(\frac{\partial^2 C}{\partial v^2}(20,-10)\text{,}\) we need information about the first partial \(\frac{\partial C}{\partial v}\) near \(v=20, T=-10\text{.}\) From that information, we can then estimate the rate at which \(\frac{\partial C}{\partial v}\) changes as we change wind speed \(v\text{.}\) To that end, note that using central differences yields
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial C}{\partial v}(25,-10) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{C(30,-10)-C(20,-10)}{30-20} = \frac{-39-(-35)}{10} \approx -0.4 \ \mathrm{\frac{^{\circ}F}{mph}}\\ \frac{\partial C}{\partial v}(20,-10) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{C(25,-10)-C(15,-10)}{25-15} = \frac{-37-(-32)}{10} \approx -0.5 \ \mathrm{\frac{^{\circ}F}{mph}}\\ \frac{\partial C}{\partial v}(15,-10) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{C(20,-10)-C(10,-10)}{20-10} = \frac{-35-(-28)}{10} \approx -0.7 \ \mathrm{\frac{^{\circ}F}{mph}} \end{align*}
From this, we have
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial^2 C}{\partial v^2}(20,-10) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{\frac{\partial C}{\partial v}(25,-10) - \frac{\partial C}{\partial v}(15,-10)}{25-15} \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{-0.4-(-0.7)}{10} \\ = \mathstrut \amp 0.03 \ \mathrm{(^{\circ}F/mph)/mph} \end{align*}
