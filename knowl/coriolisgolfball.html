Checkpoint 2.3.27. Coriolis Force on a Golf Ball.

We are on the surface of the Earth at a golf course in Saint Cloud, Minnesota (latitude of \(\varphi = 45.6^{\circ}\)). We hit a golf ball due south with speed \(v\text{.}\)
  1. Explain why the magnitude of the Coriolis force \(\|\vec{F}_{\rm{Cor}}\|\) on the golf ball has value
    \begin{equation*} 2mv\Omega \sin\varphi. \end{equation*}
  2. The Coriolis effect is very slight. To see this, calculate the magnitude of the force on the golf ball \(\vec{F}_g\) due to gravity and compare it (as a percentage) to the magnitude of the Coriolis force \(\vec{F}_{\rm{Cor}}\text{.}\) Assume the mass of the golf ball is \(m=45\) g and travels at a typical ball speed of \(v=\) 50 m/s.
