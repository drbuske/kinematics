Understanding circulation in three dimensions may be easier with the help of a paddlewheel (see FigureÂ 4.2.14). Let \(\vec{v}(x,y,z)\) be a vector field and imagine placing a tiny paddlewheel in the vector field at the point \((x, y, z)\) so that the handle points in the direction of the axis that you want to measure the circulation of \(\vec{v}\) around.
  • The angular velocity of the paddlewheel measures the strength of the circulation.
  • The circulation is positive if the rotation is counterclockwise and negative if the rotation is clockwise (as you look down the handle of the paddlewheel).
