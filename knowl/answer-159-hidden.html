Using forward differences, we have
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(A) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{f(E)-f(A)}{1}\\ \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(E) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{f(B)-f(E)}{1} \end{align*}
From this, we note that
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2}(E) \approx \frac{\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(E)-\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(A)}{1} \approx f(B)-2f(E)+f(A). \end{equation*}
Similarly,
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}(E) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{f(C)-f(E)}{1}\\ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}(D) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{f(E)-f(D)}{1} \end{align*}
gives
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y^2}(E) \approx \frac{\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}(E)-\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}(D)}{1} \approx f(C)-2f(E)+f(D). \end{equation*}
From this, using backward differences we have
\begin{equation*} \Delta f(E) = \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2}(E) + \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y^2}(E) \approx f(A) + f(B)+f(C)+f(D)-4f(E). \end{equation*}
