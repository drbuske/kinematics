Temperatures \(T\) (in \(^{\circ}C\)) at locations \(x\) km east and \(y\) km north of Middletown are shown in Figure 3.2.3.

We estimate \(\frac{\partial T}{\partial x}(A)\) by looking at information to the left and to the right of \(A\text{.}\) So, \(\frac{\partial T}{\partial x}(A) \approx \frac{(24-22)^{\circ}C}{(4-2)km} = 1 \frac{^{\circ}C}{km}\text{.}\) Similarly, \(\frac{\partial T}{\partial y}(A) \approx \frac{(24-21)^{\circ}C}{(3-1)km} = 1.5 \frac{^{\circ}C}{km}\text{.}\) The gradient at \(A\text{,}\) the vector represented by the arrow in Figure 3.2.3, can then be written as

\begin{equation*} \nabla T(A) \approx 1 \frac{^{\circ}C}{km}\vec{i} + 1.5\frac{^{\circ}C}{km}\vec{j}. \end{equation*}
