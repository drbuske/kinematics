For some unit vector \(\vec{n}\) perpendicular to both \(2\vec{j}+\vec{k}\) and \(\vec{i}\) we have
\begin{align*} (2\vec{j}+\vec{k}) \times \vec{i} = \mathstrut \amp \left( \| 2\vec{j}+\vec{k} \| \cdot \| \vec{i} \| \cdot \sin 90^{\circ} \right) \vec{n} \\ = \mathstrut \amp \sqrt{5} \cdot 1 \cdot 1 \cdot \vec{n}. \end{align*}
Rather than find this vector \(\vec{n}\text{,}\) we can use properties to compute the cross product. Since \(\vec{j} \times \vec{i} = -\vec{k}\) and \(\vec{k} \times \vec{i} = \vec{j}\text{,}\) we see that \((2\vec{j}+\vec{k}) \times \vec{i} = -2\vec{k} +\vec{j}\text{.}\) This vector is shown in FigureÂ 2.3.14.
