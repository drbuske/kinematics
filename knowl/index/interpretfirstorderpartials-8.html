Checkpoint 5.1.23. Current Wind Chill Index Formula.

In Minnesota, we tend to report air temperature in degrees Fahrenheit and wind speed in miles per hour. Using these units of measure, the current formula for the wind chill index is
\begin{equation*} C(w,T) = 35.74 + 0.6215T -35.75w^{0.16} + 0.4275Tw^{0.16} \end{equation*}
where \(w\) is the wind speed in miles per hour (mph) and \(T\) is the air temperature in degees Fahrenheit (\(^{\circ}F\)). The domain for this function only includes wind speeds \(w\) above 3 mph and air temperatures \(T\) below \(50^{\circ}F\text{.}\)
  1. Evaluate \(C(10,25)\text{.}\) Compare this value to that given in Table 5.1.21.
  2. Compute \(\frac{\partial C}{\partial w}(w,T)\) and \(\frac{\partial C}{\partial T}(w,T)\text{.}\)
  3. Evaluate \(\frac{\partial C}{\partial w}(10,25)\text{.}\) Describe the meaning of this in practical terms. Then, compare your answer to that in Checkpoint 5.1.20(a).
Answer.
  1. \(C(10,25) \approx 15.05^{\circ}F\) which is quite similar to the value of \(15^{\circ}F\) found in Table 5.1.21.
  2. \(\displaystyle \frac{\partial C}{\partial w} = -\frac{5.72}{w^{0.84}} + \frac{0.0684T}{w^{0.84}}\text{;}\) \(\displaystyle \frac{\partial C}{\partial T} = 0.6215 + 0.4275w^{0.16}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \frac{\partial C}{\partial w}(10,25) \approx -0.58^{\circ}F/mph\text{;}\) In practical terms, this gives the rate at which the wind chill is changing when the wind speed is \(w=10\) mph and the air temperature is \(T=25^{\circ}F\text{.}\) Under those conditions, when the velocity of the wind increases 1 mph, we can expect the feels like temperature (i.e. wind chill) to decrease by about 0.6 of a degree Fahrenheit even if the air temperature remains constant. This is roughly the same as in Checkpoint 5.1.20(a).
