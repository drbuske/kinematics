Exercise 14. Insolation.

The acronym insolation means incoming solar radiation at the top of the atmosphere. Figure 5.1.27 shows the incoming solar radiation \(\bar{E}\text{,}\) measured in \(W/m^2\text{,}\) as a function of latitude and day of the year. That is, it gives the contours of \(\bar{E}(d, \phi)\) where \(d\) is the day of the year (after January 1) and \(\phi\) is the latitude in degrees north of the equator.
  1. Use the contour map to estimate the value of \(\bar{E}(80,45.6^{\circ})\text{.}\) Be sure to include units. Then, interpret the meaning of this value knowing that St. Cloud, MN lies at this latitude.
  2. Is \(\frac{\partial \bar{E}}{\partial d}(80,45.6^{\circ})\) positive, negative, or zero? How does the contour diagram tell you this? How does common sense tell you this?
  3. Is \(\frac{\partial \bar{E}}{\partial \phi}(80,45.6^{\circ})\) positive, negative, or zero? What does this suggest for the travel plans of college students over spring break?
  4. Determine values of \(d_0\) and \(\phi_0\) such that both \(\frac{\partial \bar{E}}{\partial d} (d_0,\phi_0) < 0\) and \(\phi_0 < 0\text{.}\) Interpret what these two conditions mean in terms of when and where one would be on the planet.
