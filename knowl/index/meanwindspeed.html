Exercise 10. Mean Wind Speed.

The mean wind speed \(M\) is a function of \(U\text{,}\) the eastward wind speed, and \(V\text{,}\) the northward wind speed. In fact,
\begin{equation*} M = \sqrt{U^2 + V^2}. \end{equation*}
  1. If the eastward wind speed is \(U=3\) m/sec and the northward wind speed is \(V=4\) m/sec, determine the mean wind speed \(M\text{.}\)
  2. Compute \(\frac{\partial M}{\partial U}\) when \(U = 3\) m/sec and \(V = 4\) m/sec. Be sure the state units with your response.
  3. Interpret the meaning of \(\frac{\partial M}{\partial U}(3,4)\) computed in (b).
Answer.
  1. 5 m/sec
  2. \(\frac{\partial M}{\partial U} = \frac{U}{\sqrt{U^2 + V^2}}\) so that \(\frac{\partial M}{\partial U}(3,4)= \frac{3}{3^2 + 4^2} = \frac{3}{5}\) which is unitless. To see this, note that units are those of \(M\) over those of \(U\) or (m/sec)/(m/sec).
  3. \(\frac{\partial M}{\partial U}(3,4)\) is the rate at which the mean wind speed is changing with respect to changes in the eastward wind speed at the moment the eastward wind speed is 3 m/sec and the northward wind speed is 4 m/sec.
