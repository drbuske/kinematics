In this section, we use the flux out of a closed surface surrounding a point to measure the outflow per unit volume there, also called the divergence, or flux density. To measure the outflow per unit volume of a vector field at a point, we calculate the flux out of a small sphere (or cube) centered at the point, divide by the volume enclosed by the sphere (or cube), then take the limit of this flux-to-volume ratio as the sphere (or cube) contracts around the point.
