Exercise 11. Saturated Vapor Pressure.

The saturated vapor pressure, \(S(T,R)\) (in millibars) is the difference between the theoretical pressure exerted by the water vapor held in saturated air (100% relative humidity \(R\) at a given temperature \(T\) in degrees Celsius) and the pressure exerted by the water vapor that is actually held in the air being measured at the same given temperature.
  1. Use TableÂ 5.1.25 to estimate the value of \(\frac{\partial S}{\partial T}(20,90)\text{.}\) What are the units of \(\frac{\partial S}{\partial T}\text{?}\)
  2. What are the units of \(\frac{\partial S}{\partial R}\text{?}\)
  3. Is \(\frac{\partial S}{\partial R}\) generally positive or negative? Explain.
Answer.
  1. \(\displaystyle \frac{\partial S}{\partial T}(20,90) \approx \frac{22.4-19.8}{21-19} = 1.3 \frac{\rm{mb}}{^{\circ}C}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \frac{\rm{mb}}{\rm{percent}}\)
  3. \(\frac{\partial S}{\partial R}\) is generally positive; for a constant temperature \(T\text{,}\) the value of \(S\) increases as \(R\) increases.
