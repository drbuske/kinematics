Exercise 15. Frostbite.

The number of minutes \(M\) until frostbite occurs is a function \(f\) of \(v\text{,}\) the wind speed (in mph), and \(T\text{,}\) the air temperature (in \(^{\circ}F\)). The table in Figure 5.1.28 gives several values of \(f\text{,}\) the minutes to frostbite under varying conditions.
  1. Compute \(M=f(25,-20)\text{.}\) Then, carefully interpret the meaning of this equality with a single sentence.
  2. Estimate \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial v}(25,-20)\text{.}\) Be sure to give units.
  3. Interpret the meaning of \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial v}(25,-20)\text{.}\)
Answer.
  1. \(f(25,-20)=7\text{.}\) This means that when the wind speed is 25 mph and the air temperature is -20 degrees Fahrenheit, frostbit will happen in 7 minutes.
  2. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial v}(25,-20) \approx \frac{6-8}{30-20} = -0.2\) minutes per mph.
  3. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial v}(25,-20) = -0.2\) minutes per mph means that when the temperature is -20\(^{\circ}F\) and the wind speed is 25 mph, the rate at which frostbite occurs is reduced by 0.2 minutes for every 1 mph increase in wind speed.
