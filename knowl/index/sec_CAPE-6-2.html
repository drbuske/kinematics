One alternative to left, right and midpoint Riemann sums (\(L_n\text{,}\) \(R_n\text{,}\) and \(M_n\)) in computing area under a curve is called the Trapezoid Rule. Rather than using a rectangle to estimate the (signed) area bounded by \(y=f(x)\) on a small interval, we use a trapezoid.
