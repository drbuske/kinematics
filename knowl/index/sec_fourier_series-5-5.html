As in linear regression, a linear system of equations (the so-called normal equations) can be solved to determine optimal values of \(a_0, a_1\text{,}\) and \(b_1\text{.}\) As in (2.2), we can write this as a linear system:
\begin{equation} \left[ \begin{array}{ccc} N \amp \sum \cos(\omega t_n) \amp \sum \sin(\omega t_n) \\ \sum \cos(\omega t_n) \amp \sum \cos^2(\omega t_n) \amp \sum \cos(\omega t_n) \sin(\omega t_n) \\ \sum \sin(\omega t_n) \amp \sum \cos(\omega t_n)\sin(\omega t_n) \amp \sum \sin^2(\omega t_n) \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} a_0 \\ a_1 \\ b_1 \end{array}\right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c} \sum y_n \\ \sum y_n \cos(\omega t_n) \\ \sum y_n \sin(\omega t_n) \end{array} \right]\tag{2.4} \end{equation}
