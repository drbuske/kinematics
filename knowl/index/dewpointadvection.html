Exercise 8. Moisture advection.

Moisture advection is horizontal transport of moisture, which plays a very important role in the development of precipitation. The advection of dew point itself can be thought as moisture advection:
\begin{equation*} Adv(T_d) = -\vec{v}_H \cdot \nabla T_d. \end{equation*}
Here \(\vec{v}_H\) is the horizontal wind vector and \(T_d(x,y)\) represents the dewpoint temperature at location \((x,y)\text{.}\) FigureÂ 5.2.11 shows current dewpoint temperatures observed near St. Cloud, MN. Suppose the horizontal wind vector (with units of m/s) is \(\vec{v}_H = 8\vec{i} +4 \vec{j}.\) Estimate the moisture advection at St. Cloud. Be sure to include units in your response. Then, interpret the meaning of this value.
Answer.
Here,
\begin{align*} \nabla T_d \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{(24.0-25.1)^{\circ}C}{34,000m} \vec{i} + \frac{(25.7-23.8)^{\circ}C}{43,000m}\vec{j}. \end{align*}
So,
\begin{align*} Adv(T_d) = \mathstrut \amp -\vec{v}_H \cdot \nabla T_d\\ = \mathstrut \amp \left( 8 \ \frac{m}{\sec} \frac{(-1.1)}{34,000} \frac{^{\circ}C}{m} + 4 \frac{m}{\sec} \frac{(1.9)}{43,000} \frac{^{\circ}C}{m} \right) \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp -0.00008208\frac{^{\circ}{C}}{\sec}. \end{align*}
This is the rate at which we would expect the dewpoint temperature at St. Cloud to change due to moisture advection.
