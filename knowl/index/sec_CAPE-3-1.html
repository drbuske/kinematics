CAPE, or Convective Available Potential Energy, is a meteorological concept used to assess the potential for thunderstorm development and intensity. CAPE is essentially a measure of how much energy is available for rising air parcels within the atmosphere. The more energy available, the stronger the potential updraft and the more intense the thunderstorm can be. Higher CAPE values generally correspond to a greater likelihood of severe weather like hail, strong winds, and tornadoes.
