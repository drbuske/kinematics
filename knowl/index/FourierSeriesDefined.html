Fourier Series Defined.

When Fourier series are applied to periodic physical phenomena having period \(T\text{,}\) it is often convenient to work in terms of the angular frequency \(\omega_0\) defined as
\begin{equation*} \omega_0 = \frac{2\pi}{T} \end{equation*}
where \(1/T = \omega_0/2\pi\) measures the number of cycles occuring in one time unit.

Example 1.2.2. Geopotential Height is Periodic.

The period for geopotential height data is \(T=360^{\circ}\) since, when on any line of latitude, moving that many degrees longitude to the east will yield the same height data. The angular frequency \(\omega_0 = \frac{2\pi}{360^{\circ}} = 1\) in this case.

Checkpoint 1.2.3.

Temperature data for Minneapolis, MN has period \(T = 365\) days (actually this value is closer to 365.25 days given leap year considerations). What is the angular frequency \(\omega_0\) for such temperatures?
Answer.
\(\omega_0 = \frac{2\pi}{365}\)
