Checkpoint 5.3.10. A Dropping Wind Chill.

The wind chill index is modeled by the function
\begin{equation*} W(T,v) = 13.12 + 0.6215T - 11.37v^{0.16} + 0.3965Tv^{0.16} \end{equation*}
where \(T\) is the temperature in degrees Celsius and \(v\) is the wind speed (in km/hr). The temperature \(T\) and the wind speed \(v\) are also, of course, functions of time \(t\text{.}\) Suppose that at 11:00 a.m., we have \(T = -15^{\circ}C\) and \(v = 30\) km/hr. Further, suppose that \(T(t)\) is decreasing by \(^{\circ}C/hr\) and \(v(t)\) is increasing by 2 km/hr. That is, \(\frac{dT}{dt} = -4 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{hr}\) and \(\frac{dv}{dt} = 2 \ \frac{km/hr}{hr}\text{.}\) At what rate is the apparent temperature (i.e. the wind chill \(W\)) changing at 11:00 a.m.?
Answer.
By the chain rule, note that
\begin{equation*} \frac{dW}{dt} = \frac{\partial W}{\partial T}\frac{dT}{dt} + \frac{\partial W}{\partial v}\frac{dv}{dt}. \end{equation*}
Since
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial W}{\partial T}= 0.6215 + 0.3965v^{0.16} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial W}{\partial v}= (0.16)(11.37)v^{-0.84} + (0.16)(0.3965)Tv^{-0.84} \end{equation*}
we have
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial W}{\partial T}(-15,30) = \mathstrut \amp 0.6215+0.3965(30)^{0.16}\\ \approx \mathstrut \amp 1.305 \end{align*}
and
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial W}{\partial v}(-15,30) = \mathstrut \amp (0.16)(11.37)(30)^{-0.84} + (0.16)(0.3965)(-15)(30)^{-0.84}\\ \approx \mathstrut \amp 0.05. \end{align*}
So at 11:00 a.m. (when \(T = -15^{\circ}C\) and \(v = 30\) km/hr) we have
\begin{align*} \frac{dW}{dt} = \mathstrut \amp \frac{\partial W}{\partial T}\frac{dT}{dt} + \frac{\partial W}{\partial v}\frac{dv}{dt} \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp \left(1.305 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{^{\circ}C} \right) \left( -4 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{hr}\right) + \left( 0.05 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{km/hr} \right) \left( 2 \ \frac{km/hr}{hr}\right) \\ = \mathstrut \amp -5.22 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{hr} + 0.1 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{hr} \\ = \mathstrut \amp -5.12 \ \frac{^{\circ}C}{hr}. \end{align*}
This means that the wind chill (or feels like temperature) is dropping at a rate of 5.12 degrees Celsius per hour at 11:00 a.m.. Most of the contribution to this drop is coming from the rapid drop in ambient temperature \(T\text{.}\)
