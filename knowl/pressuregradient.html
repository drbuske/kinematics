Example 4.1.15. Pressure Gradients.

Using isobars for atmospheric pressure \(p(x,y)\) at location \((x,y)\text{,}\) one can see direction of the pressure gradient at several points in FigureÂ 4.1.16. The gradient is the vector that points in the direction of steepest increase. Its magnitude is the rate of increase in that direction.
The magnitude of the gradient at a point is estimated by dividing the contour interval by the shortest distance between contours across that point. Here,
\begin{equation*} \| \nabla p \| \approx \frac{1012 \ mb - 1008 \ mb}{100 \ km} = 0.04 \ mb/km. \end{equation*}
