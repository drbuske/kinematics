To start, we estimate
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial T}{\partial x}(A) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{-0.6^{\circ}C}{66 \ {\rm{km}}}\\ \frac{\partial T}{\partial x}(B) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{-0.3^{\circ}C}{87 \ {\rm{km}}} \end{align*}
which gives
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial^2 T}{\partial x^2}({\rm{Rochester}}) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{\frac{\partial T}{\partial x}(B)-\frac{\partial T}{\partial x}(A)}{(33+43.5) \ {\rm{km}}}\\ = \mathstrut \amp 7.376 \times 10^{-5} \frac{^{\circ}C}{\rm{km}^2} \end{align*}
The positive second-order partial tells us that the temperature is decreasing less as we move to the east (a concave up temperature profile).
