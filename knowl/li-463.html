Item 4.6.6.c.
To estimate the mixed partial, we use values
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial I}{\partial H}(94,75) \approx \frac{127-122}{80-75} = 1 \frac{^{\circ}F}{\%} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial I}{\partial H}(92,75) \approx \frac{119-115}{80-75} = 0.8 \frac{^{\circ}F}{\%}. \end{equation*}
Then,
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial^2 I}{\partial T \partial H}(94,75) \approx \frac{\frac{\partial I}{\partial H}(94,75)-\frac{\partial I}{\partial H}(92,75)}{94-92} \approx \frac{1-0.8}{2} = 0.1 \frac{^{\circ}F/\%}{^{\circ}F}. \end{equation*}
When the temperature is 94 degrees and the relative humidity is 75%, this value tells us how fast the rate of change of the heat index with respect to a change in humidity is increasing as the temperature increases.
