Item 3.1.20.a.
\(\frac{\partial C}{\partial w}(10,25) \approx \frac{13-19}{15-5} = -0.6 \frac{^{\circ}F}{\rm{mph}}\text{;}\) in practical terms, this means that when the wind is blowing at 10 mph and the temperature is \(25^{\circ}F\text{,}\) then it will feel roughly -0.6 degrees colder for every 1 mph increase in wind speed. That is, this is the rate as which the windchill changes with respect to a change in wind speed.
