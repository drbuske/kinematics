If Sally runs twice as fast as Patty and Patty runs five times as fast as Joey, then Sally runs ten times faster than Joey. Mathematically, if \(S\) = Sally's position, \(P\) = Patty's position, and \(J\) = Joey's position, then
\begin{equation*} \frac{dS}{dJ} = \left(\frac{dS}{dP} \right) \left(\frac{dP}{dJ} \right) = (2)(5)=10 . \end{equation*}
