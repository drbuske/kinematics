We will restrict our movement to the plane \(y=2\text{.}\) The intersection of these two surfaces is a space curve - the parabola \(z=5-x^2\) parallel to the \(xz-\)plane. The slope of this curve at \((1,2,4)\) is the same as the slope of the parabola \(z=5-x^2\) at \((1,4)\text{.}\) Using calculus, thd slope of this parabola at \((x_0,z_0)=(1,4)\) is \(\frac{dz}{dx}=-2\text{.}\) This means that at the point \((1,2,4)\) on the surface, our rate of change in height, \(z\text{,}\) is \(-2\) as we change \(x\text{.}\) We denote this fact by writing \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(1,2) = -2\) and saying that the partial derivative of \(f(x,y)\) with respect to \(x\) at \((1,2)\) is \(-2\text{.}\)
