The vertical wind shear \(\vec{v}_s\) between the surface and a height of \(z=6\) km is a standard tool in storm prediction. When the vertical distance between layers is fixed (as it is here at 6 km), just the difference of the wind \(\vec{v}_2\) at the upper layer \(z=6\) km and the wind \(\vec{v}_1\) at the surface called the bulk shear is used. The bulk shear is the wind difference over a layer calculated by vector subtraction. A tool that illustrates and calculates bulk shear between two layers can be found at https://courseware.e-education.psu.edu/courses/meteo361/javascript/Lesson3/shear_calc.html# 34 .
