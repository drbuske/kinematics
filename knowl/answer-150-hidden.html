To estimate the second-order partial \(\frac{\partial^2 C}{\partial T^2}(20,-10)\text{,}\) collect information about the first partial \(\frac{\partial C}{\partial T}\text{.}\) We then estimate the rate at which \(\frac{\partial C}{\partial T}\) changes as we change temperature \(T\text{.}\) Central differences yield
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial C}{\partial T}(20,-15) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{C(20,-10)-C(20,-20)}{-10-(-20)} = \frac{-35-(-48)}{10} \approx 1.3 \ \mathrm{\frac{^{\circ}F}{^{\circ}F}}\\ \frac{\partial C}{\partial T}(20,-10) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{C(20,-15)-C(20,-5)}{-15-(-5)} = \frac{-42-(-29)}{-10} \approx 1.3 \ \mathrm{\frac{^{\circ}F}{^{\circ}F}}\\ \frac{\partial C}{\partial T}(20,-5) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{C(20,-10)-C(20,0)}{-10-0} = \frac{-35-(-22)}{-10} \approx 1.3 \ \mathrm{\frac{^{\circ}F}{^{\circ}F}} \end{align*}
So,
\begin{align*} \frac{\partial^2 C}{\partial T^2}(20,-10) \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{\frac{\partial C}{\partial T}(20,-5) - \frac{\partial C}{\partial T}(20,-15)}{-5 -(-15)}\\ \approx \mathstrut \amp \frac{1.3-1.3}{10} \\ = \mathstrut \amp 0 \ \mathrm{(^{\circ}F/^{\circ}F)/^{\circ}F}. \end{align*}
We interpret this to mean that rate of change of the wind chill with respect to temperature changes (i.e. \(\frac{\partial C}{\partial T}\)) is roughly constant when \(v=20\) mph and \(T=-10^{\circ}F\text{.}\) Thinking visually, if we graphed \(C(v,T)\) and the trace \(v=20\text{,}\) the slopes to this trace near \(T=-10\) (as in ExampleÂ 4.6.3) would all be the same.
