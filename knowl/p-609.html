The divergence, or flux density, of a smooth vector field \(\vec{F}\) is a scalar-valued function given by
\begin{gather*} div \ \vec{F} (x,y,z) = \lim_{Volume \rightarrow 0} \frac{\int_S \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A}}{\text{Volume of }S} \end{gather*}
where \(S\) is a sphere centered at \((x,y,z)\text{,}\) oriented outward, that contracts to the point \((x,y,z)\) in the limit.
