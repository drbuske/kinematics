Item 3.1.23.c.
\(\displaystyle \frac{\partial C}{\partial w}(10,25) \approx -0.58^{\circ}F/mph\text{;}\) In practical terms, this gives the rate at which the wind chill is changing when the wind speed is \(w=10\) mph and the air temperature is \(T=25^{\circ}F\text{.}\) Under those conditions, when the velocity of the wind increases 1 mph, we can expect the feels like temperature (i.e. wind chill) to decrease by about 0.6 of a degree Fahrenheit even if the air temperature remains constant.
