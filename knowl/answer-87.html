Answer 2.3.27.1.

  1. Since the angle between \(\vec{\Omega}\) and \(\vec{v}\) is \(\varphi\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{align*} \|\vec{F}_{\rm{Cor}}\| = \mathstrut \amp \|-2 m\vec{\Omega} \times m\vec{v}\| \\ = \mathstrut \amp 2m \cdot \Omega \cdot \| \vec{v} \| \cdot \sin\varphi \end{align*}
  2. We calculate \(\|\vec{F}_{\rm{Cor}}\|/\|\vec{F}_g\|\text{.}\)
    \begin{align*} \|\vec{F}_{\rm{Cor}}\| = \mathstrut \amp m \| \vec{a}_{\rm{Cor}} \| \\ = \mathstrut \amp m \cdot 2\Omega \sin\varphi (v_y\vec{i} - v_x \vec{j})\\ = \mathstrut \amp (0.045 \ {\rm{kg}}) 2(7.2921 \times 10^{-5} \ {\rm{rad/s}})\sin(45.6^{\circ}) \left[ (50 \ {\rm{m/s}})\vec{i} - 0\vec{j}\right]\\ \approx \mathstrut \amp 2.34 \times 10^{-4} \ {\rm{Newtons}} \end{align*}
    And,
    \begin{align*} \|\vec{F}_g\| = \mathstrut \amp (0.045 \ {\rm{kg}})(9.81 \ \frac{\rm{m}}{\rm{s}^2}\\ = \mathstrut \amp 0.441 \ {\rm{Newtons}} \end{align*}
    This gives \(\|\vec{F}_{\rm{Cor}}\|/\|\vec{F}_g\| \approx 5.32 \times 10^{-4}\text{.}\) That is, the Coriolis force on the golf ball is about 0.05% that of the gravitational force on the golf ball.
