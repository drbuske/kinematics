Answer 2.1.8.1.

  1. \(\vec{v} = -\vec{i} +\vec{j}\) (infinitely-many such vectors exist)
  2. Answers vary; for example, \(\vec{v} = -2\vec{i} +2\vec{j}\) (infinitely-many such vectors exist)
  3. \(\vec{w} = \vec{i} -\vec{j}\) (infinitely-many such vectors exist)
  4. \(\displaystyle \vec{u} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(-\vec{i} +\vec{j})\) (exactly one such vector exists)
