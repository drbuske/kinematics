Suppose the temperature in Jackson, MS, is \(21^{\circ}C\) and in Memphis the current temperature is \(15^{\circ}C\text{.}\) The average wind between the two cities is 20 knots from the north. The distance between Jackson and Memphis is 317,548 meters. If the only process affecting temperature is advection, and we assume the wind stays constant, we determine the temperature in Jackson in 6 hours.

Mathematically, we model the current wind using a one-dimensional vector (e.g. a scalar), \(v_{curr}\text{.}\) We know that \(| v_{curr} | = 20\) knots = 10.2 m/sec. We also know the change in time over which the advection is taking place. We convert this to appropriate units to see that \(\Delta t = 6\) hr = 21,600 sec.

So,

\begin{align*} \displaystyle T_{fcst} = \mathstrut \amp T_{curr} + \Delta t | v_{curr} | \left( \frac{T_{upwind} - T_{downwind}}{\Delta x} \right) \\ = \mathstrut \amp 21^{\circ}C + (21,600 \ s)(10.2 \ m/s)\left( \frac{16^{\circ}C - 21^{\circ}C}{317,548 \ m} \right) \\ \approx \mathstrut \amp 17.5^{\circ}C. \end{align*}
