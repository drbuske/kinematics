\(\displaystyle \nabla_H \cdot \vec{v} = \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial v}{\partial y} \approx \frac{\Delta u}{\Delta x} + \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta y}\text{.}\) Since the east-west component of the wind (i.e. \(u\)) is not changing as we move to the east, we have \(\displaystyle \frac{\Delta u}{\Delta x}=0\text{.}\) Similarly, since the north-south component of the wind (i.e. \(v=0\)) is not changing as we move to the north, we also have \(\displaystyle \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta y}=0\text{.}\) It follows that the horizontal divergence \(\nabla_H \cdot \vec{v} = 0\text{.}\)
