1. The level sets of a linear function of one variable are equally spaced points on the \(x\)-axis (i.e. in the domain space). See also Figure 1.3.11.
  2. The level sets of a linear function in two variables are parallel, equally spaced lines. See also Figure 1.3.12.
  3. The level sets of a linear function in three variables are parallel, equally spaced planes in space.
