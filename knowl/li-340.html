Item 3.1.3.5.c.
The value of \(p\) is larger at Minneapolis than at Washington, DC. The value of \(p\) is more than 1016 millibars at Minneapolis and is far lower than that at Wasington, DC. The value of \(\frac{\partial p}{\partial x}\) at Washington, DC is much larger than the value of \(\frac{\partial p}{\partial x}\) at Minneapolis. The rate at which the pressures are changing as one moves slightly east (in the positive \(x\) direction) is much greater at Washington, DC since the isobars are closer together.
