Given coordinates \((a,b,c)\) we can locate a point \(P\) as shown in Figure 1.2.3 (see also www.geogebra.org/m/rA4fWtKH 12 ). We can also think of the coordinates as instructions telling you how to get to the point. To get to point \(P\text{,}\) start at the origin, go \(a\) units along the \(x\)-axis, then \(b\) units in the direction parallel to the \(y\)-axis, and finally \(c\) units in the direction parallel to the \(z\)-axis.
