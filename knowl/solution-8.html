Solution 2.2.1.1.

\(\vec w\) in the direction of the airplane. A direction vector for the airplane is \(\vec v = \mathit{\vec i}+\mathit{\vec j}\text{.}\) The component of \(\vec w_1\) in the direction of \(\vec v\) is given by
\begin{equation*} {{\vec w_1 \cdot \vec v} \over ||{\vec v}}|| = {-2 \over \sqrt{2}} \approx -1.41. \end{equation*}
Similarly, we find the component for each wind vector in the direction of the airplane. We see that, in the direction of the airplane, the component of \(\vec w_1\) is -1.41, of \(\vec w_2\) is 2.12, of \(\vec w_3\) is -7.07, of \(\vec w_4\) is 6.36, and of \(\vec w_5\) is 4.95. Thus, the vector \(\vec w_4\) increases the plane's speed the most and the vector \(\vec w_3\) slows the plane down the most.
