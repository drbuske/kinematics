Example 1.1.10. \(\vec{i} \times (\vec{i} + \vec{j})\) - Part II.

Another way to compute \(\vec{i} \times (\vec{i} + \vec{j})\) (see ExampleÂ 1.1.8) is to recognize that the angle between these two vectors is \(45^{\circ}\text{.}\) So,
\begin{align*} \vec{i} \times (\vec{i} + \vec{j}) = \mathstrut \amp (\| \vec{i} \| \cdot \| \vec{i} + \vec{j} \| \sin 45^{\circ}) \vec{k} \\ = \mathstrut \amp 1 \cdot \sqrt{2} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \vec{k} \\ = \mathstrut \amp \vec{k}. \end{align*}
