With \(r=2\) and \(\theta=\pi/3\text{,}\) we find \(x=r\cos \theta = 2\cdot \cos(\pi/3) = 1\) and \(y=r\sin \theta = 2\cdot \sin(\pi/3) = \sqrt{3}\text{.}\) Thus, for \((r,\theta) = (2, \pi/3)\text{,}\) the rectangular coordinates are \((1, \sqrt{3})\text{.}\) B. With \(r=1\) and \(\theta=5\pi/3\text{,}\) we find \(x=r\cos \theta = 1\cdot \cos(5\pi/3) = { 1 \over 2}\) and \(y=r\sin \theta = 1\cdot \sin(5\pi/3) = { -1 \sqrt{3} \over 2}\text{.}\) Thus, for \((r,\theta) = (1, 5\pi/3)\text{,}\) the rectangular coordinates are \(({ 1 \over 2}, { -1 \sqrt{3} \over 2})\text{.}\)
