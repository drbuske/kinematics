Exercise 5.

Shown in Figure 4.6.21 is a contour plot of a function \(f\) with the values of \(f\) labeled on the contours. The point \((2,1)\) is highlighted in red.
  1. Estimate the partial derivatives \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(2,1)\) and \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}(2,1)\text{.}\)
  2. Determine whether the second-order partial derivative \(\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2}(2,1)\) is positive or negative, and explain your thinking.
  3. Determine whether the second-order partial derivative \(\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y^2}(2,1)\) is positive or negative, and explain your thinking.
  4. Determine whether the second-order partial derivative \(\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y \partial x}(2,1)\) is positive or negative, and explain your thinking.
  5. Determine whether the second-order partial derivative \(\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x \partial y}(2,1)\) is positive or negative, and explain your thinking.
  6. Consider a function \(g\) of the variables \(x\) and \(y\) for which \(\frac{\partial g}{\partial x}(2,2) \gt 0\) and \(\frac{\partial^2 g}{\partial x^2}(2,2) \lt 0\text{.}\) Sketch possible behavior of some contours around \((2,2)\) on the left axes in Figure 4.6.22.
  7. Consider a function \(h\) of the variables \(x\) and \(y\) for which \(\frac{\partial h}{\partial x}(2,2) \gt 0\) and \(\frac{\partial^2 h}{\partial y \partial x}(2,2) \lt 0\text{.}\) Sketch possible behavior of some contour lines around \((2,2)\) on the right axes in Figure 4.6.22.
