Item 3.1.3.6.c.
\(\frac{\partial \theta}{\partial T}(T,p) = \left( \frac{1000}{p} \right)^{0.286}\) so that for every one degree increase in absolute temperature \(T\text{,}\) the potential temperature \(\theta\) increases by \(\left( \frac{1000}{p} \right)^{0.286}\text{.}\) Note that this amount of increase does not depend on the initial temperature.
