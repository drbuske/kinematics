For the vector field \(\vec{F}\) illustrated in FigureÂ 4.5.5, the divergence at the origin is positive since there is a net flow out of that point. While a small amount of the flow appears to be moving towards the origin from the left, much more flow is moving away towards the right. If the surface of this sphere \(S\) were oriented outward, then the flux of \(\vec{F}\) across \(S\) (no matter the size of \(S\text{!}\)) would be positive. The divergence, or flux density, at the origin is then also positive.
