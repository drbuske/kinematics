Since \(17^{\circ}C = 290.15^{\circ}K\text{,}\) we have
\begin{gather*} R_{ul} = (0.97)(5.67 \times 10^{-8} \ \frac{W}{m^2 \cdot K^4})(290.15^{\circ}K)^4 \approx 389.8 \ \frac{W}{m^2} \end{gather*}
gives the longwave radiation (flux) during September. Similarly, the longwave radiation during March is found to be
\begin{gather*} R_{ul} = (0.97)(5.67 \times 10^{-8} \ \frac{W}{m^2 \cdot K^4})(274.15^{\circ}K)^4 \approx 310.7 \ \frac{W}{m^2}. \end{gather*}
