Answer 2.1.10.1.

The mathematical direction is given by \(\phi_{polar} = 90^{\circ} - \phi_{vect} = 90^{\circ} - 236^{\circ} = 214^{\circ}\text{.}\) Resolving a mathematical vector \(\phi_{polar}\) with angle \(214^{\circ}\) (with the positive \(x\)-axis) and length of \(46.3\) km/hr (the equivalent of 25 knots) yields
\begin{align*} \vec{v}_{storm} = \amp 46.3\cos(214^{\circ})\vec{i} + 46.3\sin(214^{\circ})\vec{j} \\ \approx \amp (-38.4 \ {\rm{km/hr}}) \vec{i} + (-25.9 \ {\rm{km/hr}}) \vec{j}\text{.} \end{align*}
