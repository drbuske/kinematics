A linear function of two variables is one that can be written in the form
\begin{gather*} f(x)=ax+by+c. \end{gather*}
The graphs of linear functions in two variables are planes and their level sets are equally-spaced parallel lines in the \(xy\)-plane (see FigureÂ 1.3.12). Differentiable functions of two-variables have graphs that look like a plane when we zoom in. They are locally linear at \((x,y)=(x_0,y_0)\) and, just like a plane, have two slopes - one in the \(x\)-direction and one in the \(y\)-direction.
